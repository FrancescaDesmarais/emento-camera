# Research Background

This is helpful background content for Claude for the Emento-Camera project.

### Starting Point
The Emento App is a patient-support app for people being admitted to hospital. For example, I used it for a knee surgery. A nurse ‘creates’ a new journey for a patient and then they get information specific to their operation or procedure. It should help patients better prepare and care for themselves before and after a hospital admission, while saving nurses time.  

### The Challenge
In my experience, there are still a lot of paper tools that nurses use, where content doesn’t get integrated into the digital app: 
- For example, one nurse wrote down the hospital department, time of operation, and time of fasting for me on a simple piece of paper. The information was more specific, and different from what I could see in the digital app. 
- Another example, a nurse gave me a sheet of paper with suggested doses and times for my pain medication after the surgery. This specific information was not in my app, I only got a generic “take pain meds” reminder and a picture of an example sheet of paper.  

### The Opportunity
Rather than anticipate all instances and code complex nurse and patient options into the app, what if you could take a picture of a piece of paper that a nurse gives you and the app is smart enough to integrate the data into the app?
